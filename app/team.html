    Irene Kuzatko

    Born in Caracas, Venezuela, to Croatian parents, Irene began dancing at the age of 3 and was trained by some of the most prestigious ballet teachers in Venezuela. Through her teenage years she became entirely dedicated to ballet, but at the age of 18 she decided to pursue other interests, obtaining a BA degree in Translation and Interpretation in Spanish, English, and French from the Central University of Venezuela, the country’s most renowned university. Years later she obtained another degree, this time as Lawyer, graduating Summa Cum Laude from the same Alma Mater, and thereafter two Master Degrees in Family and Children Law and Procedure Law. Nevertheless, during her studies and professional career both as translator and lawyer, her love and joy for dancing never faded and she managed to combine her professional life with ballet practice and to teach Ballet and Jazz to children and teenagers for over 15 years.


    In 2012 she decided to move with her family to Zagreb and to pursue her dream of running a dance studio, so as to recreate her magnificent experience of teaching ballet and materialize her desire to share and make the magic of dance accessible to everyone, even to children and youngsters with special needs. With this goal in mind, more recently she obtained a Specialization Degree in Teaching Fundamentals and completed two certification programs in Ballet Teaching Methods for Children with Special Needs -Psycho-Ballet (a Cuban Method) and the Schlachte™ Method (a North American Method developed by Bonnie Schlachte)-.


    Dance has been a huge part of Irene’s life and, since her own growth as an individual came from the world of Ballet, she looks forward to sharing her passion for Ballet and all the knowledge and benefits gained through this form of art.



    Dora Kokolj

    Dora Kokolj was born in Zagreb in 1989. There she enrolled and graduated at the School of Classical Ballet. After finishing High School, she enrolled in the Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences of the University of Zagreb, where she obtained a Degree in History of Art and Pedagogy. In 2014 she graduated from the Dance Academy in Skopje (Skopje Dance Academy). During and after her studies she performed with Skopje Dance Theatre, the associate company of the school, and also worked as freelance dancer, acquiring performance experience and collaborating with Croatian and foreign choreographers and artists such as Jannis Brenner (USA), Joan van der Mast (NL), Risima Risimkin (MK), Tim percent (UK / USA), Igor Kirov (MK), Katsura Kana (JPN), Nada Kokotović (HR / GER), Staša Zurovac (HR), Boris Bakal (HR) and others. Occasionally, during and after her education, she worked as a dance teacher, working with children and adults, professionals and amateurs alike.



    Sven Copony

    Born in Munich to a Croatian mother, Sven started studying Ballet quiet late, as a 17-year-old boy, taking advantage of his wide experience in street style dancing -Breakdance, Hip-Hop, Jazz…- and Martial Arts (Teakwondo). After one year at the Dance Academy in Leipzig, he was admitted to the Hochschule für Musik, a prestigious Ballet and Music Academy in Munich, where he successfully graduated in 1999.


    Already during his studies he was interested in choreography and had the opportunity to perform a creative work in collaboration with Marcella Lehner, a former Choreographer of the pop-star Prince. After refusing two job offerings from the Contemporary Dance Companies St. Gallen and Würzburg, in 2000 he decided to move to Croatia to become a member of the Croatian National Ballet (HNK) in Zagreb, where he performed a wide variety of roles in almost all dance productions until 2015. Simultaneously to his job as a HNK dancer, he frequently performed as guest soloist at the ballet troupe Croatia, the Musical Theater Komedija as well as in a wide range of music and dance performances outside the theater.

    Since 2009 he has been working as a choreographer and dance teacher at Zagreb’s Dance Center, Buena Vista Dance Club and Reforma and giving workshops from classical ballet to contemporary dance in Zadar, Zagreb, Dubrovnik, München, Heidelberg, among many other cities.

    Currently he is a member of the Dance Medicine Society of Munich and since 2015 works as a teacher in the state school for Ballet and Contemporary Dance in Zagreb.



    Patricio Agüero

    Patricio Agüero was born in Venezuela, where he graduated as Technical Bachelor from “Cristobal Rojas” School of Visual, Dance and Performance Arts and then as Bachelor in Fine Arts, major in Painting from the Armando Reveron University. In Venezuela he worked as Assistant instructor of Contemporary Dance and Corporal Expression as well as a Dance Therapy Instructor specialized in Latin Rhythms.


    When he moved to Zagreb, he did an internship in Zagreb Puppet Theater, creating and designing puppets and afterwards became the Director of Art of the Children´s theater company, Klub Kulture Agram Zagreb. He also worked as illustrator of the Spanish company “Kreatia”.


    From 2003 he has been working as Pilates, body expression and dance fitness instructor, both in Croatia and Spain.



    Sandra Sok

    Sandra Sok is a body and movement researcher, contemporary dancer, Pilates and yoga certified teacher, philosopher, thinker, globetrotter... During the last 15 years she has been teaching a bunch of physical disciplines that incorporate a holistic healthy approach by connecting body and mind.


    After many years of training, exploring, and participating in several national and internationals workshops, summer schools and certification courses - Contemporary dance, Pilates, yoga, Reiki, Fluid Balance, Capoeira, Acrobatics... - she is now actively promoting her philosophy of movement through her own programs: FlowDancing (a synthesis of several approaches inspired by dance and non-dance techniques), Swinga (a program that turns yoga into a fluid, smooth, and powerful dance movement to improve body alignment, strengthen core, develop flexibility, and enhance mental health) and , and Synergy in Motion (based on Pilates technique focused on improving balance and alignment).
