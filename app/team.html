    Irene Kuzatko (Ballet)

    Born in Caracas, Venezuela, to Croatian parents, Irene began dancing at the age of 3 and was trained by some of the most prestigious ballet teachers in Venezuela. Through her teenage years she became entirely dedicated to ballet, but at the age of 18 she decided to pursue other interests, obtaining a BA degree in Translation and Interpretation in Spanish, English, and French from the Central University of Venezuela, the country’s most renowned university. Years later she obtained another degree, this time as Lawyer, graduating Summa Cum Laude from the same Alma Mater, and thereafter two Master Degrees in Family and Children Law and Procedure Law. Nevertheless, during her studies and professional career both as translator and lawyer, her love and joy for dancing never faded and she managed to combine her professional life with ballet practice and to teach Ballet and Jazz to children and teenagers for over 15 years.


    In 2012 she decided to move with her family to Zagreb and to pursue her dream of running a dance studio, so as to recreate her magnificent experience of teaching ballet and materialize her desire to share and make the magic of dance accessible to everyone, even to children and youngsters with special needs. With this goal in mind, more recently she obtained a Specialization Degree in Teaching Fundamentals and completed two certification programs in Ballet Teaching Methods for Children with Special Needs -Psycho-Ballet (a Cuban Method) and the Schlachte™ Method (a North American Method developed by Bonnie Schlachte)-.


    Dance has been a huge part of Irene’s life and, since her own growth as an individual came from the world of Ballet, she looks forward to sharing her passion for Ballet and all the knowledge and benefits gained through this form of art.



    Sofija Široka (Latin Dance)

    Sofija Široka started her dance education in elementary school, taking creative dance and drama lessons. In 2002 she started social dancing and in 2006 she began her training in Caribbean Dance styles at Salsamania Dance Club. In 2010 she started taking Jazz Dance lessons at Action Dance Studio, enrolling in Ballet lessons a year later. Since 2009 she has been taking classes and attending numerous dance workshops with the world's most renowned Salsa teachers, gaining knowledge in a variety of Salsa styles (e.g. Salsa on1 & on2) as well as Bachata, Cha-Cha-Cha, Reggaeton, Cuban Son, Pachanga, among others. More recently, she decided to broaden her dance knowledge with different techniques such as Kizomba (2012), Acrobatics (2013) and Hip-hop (2016). In 2012 she became a member of Los Mamberos Dance Club and since then she has been teaching Salsa and Modern/Traditional Bachata with her partner and giving Salsa classes for women based on Ladies’ Styling and Jazz Dance technique. She has also conducted numerous Salsa Workshops both in Croatia and in the neighboring countries. Since 2015 she has been working as an External Associate Singer and Dancer at Komedija Municipal Theatre. Along with her daily dance and singing training and performances, Sofija is currently preparing a dance show with her partner.



    Dora Kokolj (Contemporary Dance)

    Dora Kokolj was born in Zagreb in 1989. There she enrolled and graduated at the School of Classical Ballet. After finishing High School, she enrolled in the Faculty of Humanities and Social Sciences of the University of Zagreb, where she obtained a Degree in History of Art and Pedagogy. In 2014 she graduated from the Dance Academy in Skopje (Skopje Dance Academy). During and after her studies she performed with Skopje Dance Theatre, the associate company of the school, and also worked as freelance dancer, acquiring performance experience and collaborating with Croatian and foreign choreographers and artists such as Jannis Brenner (USA), Joan van der Mast (NL), Risima Risimkin (MK), Tim percent (UK / USA), Igor Kirov (MK), Katsura Kana (JPN), Nada Kokotović (HR / GER), Staša Zurovac (HR), Boris Bakal (HR) and others. Occasionally, during and after her education, she worked as a dance teacher, working with children and adults, professionals and amateurs alike.



    Diana Nasurović (Balet/Fitness)

    Born in Zagreb. Studied dance at Ana Maletic School of Contemporary Dance, BA in Music and Media Management from London Metropolitan University and Personal Training Certificate from Australian Institute of Personal Trainers.


    After completing her dance training in Croatia, Diana spent 10 years in London and Melbourne taking and teaching dance classes, working as a Personal Trainer and a Barre instructor. Drawing from her varied fitness and dance background the classes are a combination of athleticism, flexibility and fluidity with a dash of humor. A lot of attention is put in movement precision while keeping the classes dynamic and fun. Get ready to feel the burn and get some seriously toned bodies!
