  • Attire

    In order to promote proper classroom etiquette and for being dance a discipline that requires ease of movement and a degree of decorum, adequate dance attire is required for classes. Wearing the proper clothing and footwear provides safety and allows the teacher to see the dancer's proper placement and make the required corrections safely. For said purposes, all students are encouraged to observe the following safety rules:


    • •Correct footwear should always be worn to avoid risk of injury.
    • •No student will be allowed to dance in socks or bare feet, unless otherwise indicated.
    • •No loose or baggy clothes (including T-shirts) should be worn in dance classes, unless otherwise indicated.
    • •Large jewelry may not be worn in dance classes as it can be distraction and poses a potential danger.
    • •For the safety of all dancers, eyeglasses must be attached with a sports strap.
    • •Dance shoes cannot be worn outdoors as this tracks dirt into the studio and is detrimental to the studio floors.

    Children and youngster ballet program:

    • •Tights or bobby socks, leotard and ballet shoes. Ballet skirt is optional.
    • •Hair must be worn in a bun or in a neat high ponytail if possible. Short hair must be secured with a band.
    • •Jewelry, other than small earrings, may not be worn in ballet class. The teacher may ask students to remove their jewelry until class is over.

    Adult balletic program:

    • •Any form-fitting clothing which the student can comfortably move in, such as a leotard and tights or a tank top or t-shirt with exercise pants, capris or leggings.
    • •Ballet shoes shall be worn to avoid risk of injury.
    • •Hair must be worn in a bun or in a neat high ponytail if possible. Short hair must be secured with a band.

    Adaptive ballet program:

    • •Any form-fitting clothing which the student can comfortably move in, such as a leotard and tights/bobby socks or a tank top or t-shirt with exercise pants, capris, leggings, stretchy dance shorts or shorts that are not too baggy.
    • •Ballet shoes.
    • •Ballet skirt (optional).
    • •Hair must be worn in a bun or in a neat high ponytail if possible. Short hair must be secured with a band.
    • •Jewelry, other than small earrings, may not be worn in ballet class.

    Jazz / Contemporay Dance / Danz-Llet / Latin Moves & Senses / Fiiness Programs:

    • •Any form-fitting clothing which the student can comfortably move in, such as a leotard and tights or a tank top or t-shirt with exercise pants, capris, leggings, stretchy dance shorts or shorts that are not too baggy.
    • •Jazz or ballet shoes shall be worn to avoid risk of injury.
    • •Baggy clothes and excessive jewelry is not allowed.
